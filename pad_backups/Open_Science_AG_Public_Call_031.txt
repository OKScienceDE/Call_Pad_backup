	* Ziel: Der Public Call soll dazu dienen, sich innerhalb der deutschsprachigen Open-Science-Szene auszutauschen, zu vernetzen und gemeinsame Projekt zu starten. Wir möchten diesen möglichst einmal pro Monat durchführen. Bei Rückfragen oder Anmerkungen wird gebeten, sich an die Mailingliste der AG - https://lists.okfn.org/mailman/listinfo/open-science-de - zu wenden. Vorraussetzungen gibt es keine, aber es wird empfohlen, sich mit dem Mission Statement der deutschsprachigen Open Science AG ( http://dx.doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.12326 / https://github.com/OKScienceDE/Mission_Statement ) auseinanderzusetzen
	* Hintergrund: Research results should be freely accessible to everyone on the web. But also research methods, the research process itself, raw data, the joint processing of the data, etc., are increasingly moving into the net - and even in these early stages, long before the finished article or book, openness has many advantages. This idea is behind the keyword "Open Science". Many deal with "open science" from different perspectives. Researchers are trying out new tools, companies like Facebook for Scientists are creating new offerings for this target group. Science journalists report about it, but also experiment with their own forms and are increasingly supplemented (repressed?) By Wissenschaftsblogger. Established players like data centers and academic libraries are also trying to adapt their role to the new possibilities. And so on. Listening to the topic of "Open Science" today, one quickly gets the impression that the music plays in English-speaking countries, whereas in Germany, Austria and Switzerland this is rather a niche topic. The many isolated projects and activists in German-speaking countries should be able to communicate more easily and more frequently, in an informal setting. We want to try this with a monthly one-hour open videoconference! On the occasion of the "OKFestival" in Berlin on 16.7.2014 a German speaking working group met on the topic "Open Science". The aim of the working group is the networking of assets in the field of opening up science and research (open science) and the development of legally secure framework conditions for the publication of research results. In addition, the working group is to co-ordinate cooperation with other international open-science groups and act as a contact point for researchers, institutes, civil society, business and politics on the subject of open science.
	* Who is allowed to participate? Anyone interested in Open Science! You do not need deep background knowledge and are welcome to ask questions. Especially people without a solid background in Open Science who want to join the community are welcome.
