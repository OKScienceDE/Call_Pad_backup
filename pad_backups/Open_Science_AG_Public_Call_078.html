  • Objective : The public call should serve to exchange ideas within the German-speaking open science scene, to network and to start joint projects. We would like to do this once a month if possible. If you have any questions or comments, please contact the AG's mailing list - https://mlists.okfn.de/cgi-bin/mailman/listinfo/open-science-de -. There are no prerequisites, but it is recommended to look into the mission statement of the German-speaking Open Science AG ( http://dx.doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.12326 / https://github.com/OKScienceDE/Mission_Statement )
  • background: Research results should be freely accessible to everyone on the Internet. But research methods, the research process itself, raw data, the joint processing of the data, etc. are shifting further and further into the network - and even in these early stages, i.e. long before the finished article or book, openness has many advantages. This is the idea behind the keyword "Open Science". Many people deal with "Open Science" from different perspectives. Researchers try out new tools, companies create new offers for this target group. Science journalists report on it, but also experiment with their own forms and are increasingly being supplemented (displaced?) By science bloggers. Established players such as data centers and academic libraries are also trying to adapt their role to the new possibilities. And so forth. If you ask around on the subject of "Open Science" today, you quickly get the impression: The music is played in English-speaking countries, but in Germany, Austria and Switzerland it is still a niche topic. The many isolated projects and activists in German-speaking countries should be able to exchange ideas more easily and more frequently, in an informal setting. We want to try that with a monthly one hour open video conference! A German-speaking working group met on July 16, 2014 as part of the OKFestival in Berlin on the subject of "Open Science". The aim of the working group is to network active people in the field of opening up science and research (Open Science) and to develop legally secure framework conditions for the publication of research results. In addition, the working group should coordinate the cooperation with other international open science groups and act as a contact for researchers, institutes, civil society, business and politics on the subject of open science.
  • Who can take part? Everyone who is interested in Open Science! You do not need a deep background knowledge and are welcome to ask questions. People without a solid background in Open Science who want to join the community are particularly welcome.
