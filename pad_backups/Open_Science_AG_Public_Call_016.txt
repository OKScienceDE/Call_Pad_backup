     Target : The Public Call is intended to exchange information within the German speaking  Open Science Scene to network and to start common projects. We want to do this, if possible, once per month. For any questions or comments please contact the mailing list of the AG - https://lists.okfn.org/mailman/listinfo/open-science-de . Requirements: There are none, but it is recommended, to comply with the mission statement of the German Open Science AG ( http://dx.doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.12326 / https://github.com/OKScienceDE/Mission_Statement )

     Background : Research results should be freely available to everyone on the network. But research methods, the research process itself, the raw data, the joint processing of data etc. is shifting ever further into the network - and even in these heed stages, so long before the finished article or book, openness has many advantages. This idea is behind the tag "Open Science". With "Open Science", many people are, from different angles. Researchers try new tools, the company - such as "Facebook for scientists" services - creating new services for this target group. Science journalists report on it, but also experiment with their own forms and are increasingly supplemented (repressed?) Through science bloggers. Established players such as data centers and academic libraries try their role also adapt to new opportunities. And so on. Listening to the topic "Open Science" today at, one quickly gets the impression: The music playing in the English-speaking world, in Germany, Austria and
   Switzerland, however, is likely still a niche topic. The many isolated projects and activists in the German-speaking countries should be able to easily and frequently exchange, in an informal setting. We want to try with a monthly one-hour open videoconferencing! On the subject of "Open Science" met on 16.07.2014 under the OKFestivals in Berlin a German working group. The working group is to network assets in the area of opening of Science and Research (Open Science) and the elaboration of legally secure framework for publishing research results. In addition, the Working Group is to coordinate the cooperation with other international Open Science groups and act as contacts for researchers, institutions, civil society, business and politics about Open Science.

     Who can join? All who are interested in Open Science! You do not have a deep background knowledge and are welcome to ask questions. Especially people without thorough background in Open Science, who wish to engage in the community are welcome.
